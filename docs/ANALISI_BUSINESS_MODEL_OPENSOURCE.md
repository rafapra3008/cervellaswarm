## 1. MODELLI DI BUSINESS OPEN-SOURCE (Overview)

### 1.1 Open-Core Model

**Cosa è:**
- Core gratis e open-source
- Feature premium a pagamento
- Enterprise features closed-source

**Pro:**
- Adoption rapida (community usa gratis)
- Revenue prevedibile (subscription)
- Segmentazione naturale (hobby vs business)

**Contro:**
- Rischio fork community
- Bilanciamento delicato (cosa gratis? cosa premium?)
- Community può sentirsi "tradita"

**Esempi di Successo (con numeri REALI!):**

| Azienda | Valuation/Revenue | Modello |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| **GitLab** | IPO $2.5B, $354M revenue (2021) | Freemium (free tier) + Paid tiers ($19-39/user) |
| **MongoDB** | Market cap $30B+ | Core open + Enterprise features |
| **HashiCorp** | IPO $8B+, $206M revenue | Open tools + Enterprise licensing |
| **Elastic** | IPO $2.5B, $227M revenue | Open search + Commercial features |

**Insight chiave:**
> "96% organizations increased open source use in 2025"
> "Open-Core projects achieve median $40M funding, top quartile $185M+"

---

### 1.2 SaaS Hosted Model

**Cosa è:**
- Software open-source (self-host gratis)
- Managed cloud hosting a pagamento
- Manutenzione zero per il cliente

**Pro:**
- Margini alti (70-90%)
- Revenue ricorrente prevedibile
- Frictionless (no setup complexity)

**Contro:**
- Costi infrastruttura
- Supporto 24/7 richiesto
- Competizione su pricing

**Esempi di Successo:**

| Azienda | Valuation/Revenue | Pricing |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| **Supabase** | $5B valuation, $200M funding | $25/mo → enterprise custom |
| **GitLab** | Self-managed + SaaS subscription | Dual model |
| **Redis** | Enterprise Cloud vs open-source | Premium hosting |

**Insight chiave:**
> "Supabase: Open Core + SaaS, manages cloud version for ease"
> "Free tier + paid plans = acquisition machine"

---

### 1.3 Freemium Model

**Cosa è:**
- Free tier con limiti (usage-based)
- Paid tier per power users
- Upsell based on behavior

**Pro:**
- Acquisition virale
- Low barrier to entry
- Data-driven conversion (AI-assisted in 2026!)

**Contro:**
- Costo free users (infrastructure)
- Conversion rate critica (need 5-15%)
- Risk "forever free" users

**Esempi di Successo:**

| Tool | Free Tier | Paid Tier | Conversion |
|------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **GitHub Copilot** | 2K completions/mo FREE (new!) | $10/mo Pro, $39/mo Pro+ | Massive adoption |
| **Cursor** | Limited | $20/mo ($40 teams) | $25M/month revenue! |
| **Windsurf** | Limited | $15/mo ($30 teams) | $3B valuation |

**Insight chiave:**
> "Freemium in 2026 = AI-assisted upselling, data-driven conversion"
> "Usage-based pricing: 60% of SaaS companies, growing fast"

---

### 1.4 Dual Licensing

**Cosa è:**
- Open-source license (GPL/AGPL) per open use
- Commercial license per closed-source products

**Pro:**
- Revenue da aziende che vogliono closed-source
- Community contribuisce gratis
- Segmentazione automatica

**Contro:**
- Community friction (contributor reluctance)
- Governance challenges (commercial bias)
- Risk forks

**Quando funziona:**
- Software infrastrutturale (database, tools)
- Customers molto sensibili a reciprocal licenses
- Copyleft licenses (AGPL/GPL)

**Esempi:**
- MySQL (historical)
- Qt Framework
- Ghostery

**Verdict per CervellaSwarm:**
❌ **NON adatto** - Tool developer-facing, meglio open-core + SaaS

## 6. RISKS & MITIGATION

### 6.1 Rischi

**1. Free tier troppo generoso**
- Risk: Nessuno paga mai
- Mitigation: 50 calls/mo limit (strict!)

**2. BYOK cannibalizza Sampling**
- Risk: Tutti usano BYOK, zero revenue
- Mitigation: BYOK = esperti, Sampling = mass market (segmenti diversi)

**3. Infrastructure costs**
- Risk: Free users bruciano budget
- Mitigation: Hard limits + scaling graduale

**4. Competitor copying**
- Risk: SNCP concept copiato
- Mitigation: Trade secret (closed-source core), execution speed

**5. Low conversion**
- Risk: < 5% free→paid
- Mitigation: Value proposition chiara, onboarding, analytics
